01. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This handbook establishes the standard operating procedures, conduct expectations, and operational guidelines for all personnel assigned to UNSC Office of Naval Intelligence, Section Three, Cell LOCKSTEP. All operatives are required to read, understand, and comply with the contents of this document in full.

This handbook does not supersede any active Directive issued by Section Three Command. In cases of conflict, the most recent Directive takes precedence. This handbook is subject to revision at the discretion of Cell Command.

Questions regarding the interpretation of any section should be directed to your immediate superior or to the Cell Commander. Do not attempt to resolve ambiguities unilaterally in the field.

02. CHAIN OF COMMAND

The chain of command for Cell LOCKSTEP is as follows, in descending order of authority:

  • Section Three Command — Ultimate authority. Directives from Command are inviolable.
  • Cell Commander — Designated by Section Three. Operational authority within the cell.
  • Deputy Cell Commander — Assumes full command in the absence of the Cell Commander. Standing liaison between all three units.
  • Section One Unit Lead — Commands all Section One agents. Reports to Cell Commander.
  • Section Three Unit Lead — Commands all Section Three agents. Reports to Cell Commander.
  • Orion Project Team Lead — Commands the Orion Project Spartan team on tactical matters. All strategic direction flows from the Cell Commander.
  • Section One Agents — Execute tasks under direction of the Section One Unit Lead.
  • Section Three Agents — Execute tasks under direction of the Section Three Unit Lead.
  • Orion Project Team Members — Operate under tactical direction of the Orion Team Lead.

No operative shall take unilateral action outside their assigned mandate without explicit authorization from their superior. All deviations must be reported within 24 hours via secure channel. Section One and Section Three units maintain operational separation by default — joint activity requires written authorization from the Cell Commander.

03. OPERATIVE CONDUCT STANDARDS
3.1 GENERAL CONDUCT

All operatives represent the Office of Naval Intelligence and are expected to conduct themselves with discipline, professionalism, and discretion at all times — whether in the field, at cell facilities, or in personal capacity.

3.2 PROHIBITED CONDUCT
  • Disclosure of cell identity, membership, or activities to any unauthorized party.
  • Use of ONI resources for personal benefit or unauthorized purposes.
  • Contact with media, civilian intelligence entities, or foreign nationals outside of operational cover without written authorization.
  • Retention of classified materials outside of designated secure storage.
  • Alcohol or substance use within 8 hours of an operational assignment.
  • Accessing Section Three systems from unsecured networks.
3.3 MANDATORY REPORTING

Operatives are required to report the following immediately to their handler or Cell Commander:

  • Any suspected surveillance or contact from hostile entities.
  • Compromise of cover identity or cell information.
  • Unauthorized contact from outside parties regarding ONI activities.
  • Personal circumstances that may affect operational reliability.
04. COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS

All inter-cell communications must be conducted through approved encrypted channels only. Refer to the Field Communications Guide for cipher schedules and dead-drop protocols.

CRITICAL: Any communication conducted on an unapproved channel — even for ostensibly routine matters — is a SERIOUS breach. All such breaches will be investigated and may result in reassignment or termination of operative status.

Communications with Section Three Command are to be routed through your designated handler exclusively. Direct contact with Command above your handler requires prior authorization from the Cell Commander.

Emergency contact procedures are detailed in Section 9 of this handbook.

05. COVER IDENTITY REQUIREMENTS

Operatives assigned a cover identity are required to maintain that identity consistently, both in the field and in all non-secure interactions. Cover biographies, supporting documentation, and training are provided by the Cell Logistics operative (ECHO-7).

  • Cover identities must not be discussed on secure cell channels unless operationally necessary.
  • Any challenge to a cover identity — however minor — must be reported immediately.
  • Operatives must not develop personal attachments within their cover that could compromise objectivity or mission integrity.
  • Cover identity materials are to be returned or destroyed upon assignment conclusion, per logistics instructions.
06. OPERATIONAL SECURITY (OPSEC)

Operatives are to assume that all environments are potentially hostile and that surveillance is always possible. The following OPSEC practices are mandatory:

  • Never discuss operational matters in unsecured locations.
  • Conduct counter-surveillance checks before and after all meetings.
  • Compartmentalize information — know only what is required for your assignment.
  • All digital devices used in an operational capacity must be Section Three-issued or cleared by technical staff.
  • Destroy all temporary operational materials (notes, printed documents) using approved methods.

Full OPSEC procedures are documented in the Operational Security Manual.

07. RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

Operatives are authorized to use force only as specified in the current Rules of Engagement (Amended) directive. The baseline ROE for Cell LOCKSTEP is as follows:

  • Lethal force is authorized only in defense of self or another operative when no alternative is available.
  • Collateral casualties are to be avoided at all costs. Civilian harm triggers mandatory incident reporting.
  • Detainment of hostile actors requires Cell Commander approval unless operationally urgent.
  • Any action resulting in a fatality — regardless of circumstances — must be documented in a full incident report within 48 hours.
  • Proactive elimination operations require written authorization from Section Three Command. Verbal authorization is not sufficient.
NOTE: Active Directives may expand or restrict the above ROE. Always confirm current standing orders before an operation.
08. ASSET HANDLING

Assets are to be treated as valuable but inherently unreliable. The following standards apply to all asset relationships:

  • Assets are to be compartmentalized — no asset should be aware of other assets unless operationally essential.
  • Asset welfare is a secondary consideration to operational security and mission success.
  • Promises made to assets must be approved by Cell Commander before being offered.
  • Asset compromise is to be reported immediately. In some circumstances, asset termination may be authorized per Directive 7-ALPHA-29 SECTION 4.2.
09. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
9.1 COMPROMISE PROTOCOL

If an operative believes their cover or the cell has been compromised, they are to:

  • Cease all operational activity immediately.
  • Activate the emergency signal per the Communications Guide.
  • Move to the designated safe house (coordinates held by handler only).
  • Await contact from Cell Commander or designated handler. Do not attempt independent exfiltration.
9.2 EXTRACTION

Emergency extraction is available 24/7 via the handler line. The extraction protocol is detailed in the Field Communications Guide. Authentication codes are issued individually and are to be memorized — never written down.

9.3 CAPTURE PROTOCOL

In the event of capture by a hostile entity, operatives are to:

  • Maintain cover identity for as long as operationally prudent.
  • Divulge no information beyond cover identity details.
  • Activate the emergency beacon if possible (device issued at briefing).
  • Be advised: Section Three does not negotiate for captured operatives. Extraction will be attempted by operational means only.
10. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

The following reports are mandatory for all operatives:

  • Daily Sitrep — Submitted via secure channel each 0800 UTC during active operations.
  • After-Action Report — Required within 72 hours of any completed operation. Template available in the Document Library.
  • Incident Report — Required within 24 hours of any use of force, cover challenge, or security anomaly.
  • Asset Contact Report — Following any meeting with a handled asset.

Failure to submit required reports is a disciplinary matter and may result in suspension of operational status pending review.
